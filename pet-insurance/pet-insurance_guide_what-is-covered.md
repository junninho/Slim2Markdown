### What's Covered?

## **So, what does pet insurance cover?**

Here‚Äôs the answer that everybody hates: it depends.

What‚Äôs covered and what's excluded all depends on the type of policy you get. But we can tell you what you should get in terms of coverage, and what to watch out for. A little self-promotion: at PolicyGenius, we only offer comprehensive plans that cover: accidents, illnesses, congenital & hereditary conditions, and chronic diseases like cancer. When you're ready to shop for pet insurance, do it with us to avoid any "gotchas."

_Ôì°_**Accident-Only Plans**

#### **Don‚Äôt bother.**  
We mentioned in Part 1 of this guide that there are accident-only pet insurance plans available. They cover vet expenses to treat accidents but not illness or disease. These are usually the least expensive type of insurance plan.

#### Our advice: don‚Äôt buy one. It‚Äôs a better use of your money‚Äîand you‚Äôll be much more satisfied with your plan in the long run‚Äîif you buy a comprehensive policy that covers accidents and illnesses.

_Ôìê_**Congenital and Hereditary Conditions**

#### **Make sure they‚Äôre covered.**  
You‚Äôll probably see these terms referred to when you‚Äôre shopping for pet insurance. Here's what they mean:

    

  * **A hereditary condition** is one that is linked to genetics ‚Äì meaning it was passed down from your pet‚Äôs parents. Example hereditary conditions include diabetes, cherry eye and hip dysplasia.
  * **A congenital condition** is not linked to genetics. It‚Äôs related to things that happened while your pet was still developing in utero. Examples of congenital conditions include heart defects, cleft palate, and defects in other organs or limbs.



Some conditions might be both congenital and hereditary. The tricky thing about congenital and hereditary conditions is that they may not appear for years, so you wouldn‚Äôt know your otherwise healthy pet has one until it shows up‚Äîand treatment is often expensive. For this reason, we encourage pet insurance buyers to consider only policies that cover congenital and hereditary conditions.

But wait‚Äîyou might think that you don‚Äôt need this coverage if your pet‚Äôs breed isn‚Äôt prone to these types of conditions. It‚Äôs true that certain breeds have higher risks for these conditions; however, that doesn‚Äôt mean that your pet, if not one of these breeds, is risk-free.

Also, policies that exclude coverage for congenital and hereditary conditions give the insurance company more ‚Äúwiggle room‚Äù to decide that a certain health condition, when the cause is unknown and difficult to determine, is congenital or hereditary. **That means your claim would be denied.**

To avoid these situations, and to make sure your pet is covered, we recommend getting a policy that covers congenital _and_ hereditary conditions.

_üíä_**Cancer &amp; Chronic Diseases**

#### **Make sure they‚Äôre covered.**  
Sadly, cancer is common in dogs and cats. It‚Äôs the leading cause of death in dogs over the age of 10. However, the good news is that half of all cancers are curable if caught early. Make sure your policy covers cancer and doesn‚Äôt place a limit on the amount you can be reimbursed for cancer treatment expenses (cancer would likely be treated as a single incident on a policy that has a per-incident limit).
